GST Implications on the Sale and Purchase of Used Cars

Key Provisions and Understanding

Margin Scheme (Rule 32(5), CGST Rules, 2017):

Registered dealers engaged in the buying and selling of second-hand cars have the option to use the Margin Scheme. Under this scheme:

  • GST is levied only on the positive margin (sale price exceeding the purchase price).
  • Input Tax Credit (ITC) cannot be availed on the purchase of such used cars.

Exemption for Intra-State Purchases by Registered Dealers

Intra-state sales of used cars by unregistered persons to registered dealers dealing in second-hand cars under the Margin Scheme are exempt from GST.

Scenarios and GST Implications

1. Sale Between Mukesh and Dinesh (Both Unregistered Persons):

Intra-State Sale: No GST implications arise since both parties are unregistered.

Inter-State Sale: If Mukesh is selling the car in the course of business, Section 24 of the CGST Act, 2017, may apply, requiring Mukesh to register under GST and charge 18% GST. However, if Mukesh sells the car as a personal asset, GST is not applicable.

2. Sale from Unregistered Individual to Registered Dealer (e.g., Mukesh to Spinny):

Intra-State Sale: No GST is charged by Mukesh as the supplier is unregistered, and the sale falls under the exemption provided in Notification No. 10/2017.

Inter-State Sale: GST does not apply even in inter-state sales since:

  • Mukesh is unregistered, and
  • Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM) is not applicable as RCM on such transactions has not been notified by CBIC.

3. Sale by Registered Dealer (e.g., Spinny)

Sale to Unregistered Individual (e.g., Dinesh):

  • Opt for Margin Scheme: GST @ 18% is collected only on the positive margin (sale price â€“ purchase price).
  • Opt-out of Margin Scheme: GST @ 18% is levied on the entire sale price.

Sale to Another Registered Dealer (e.g., Cars24):

  • If Spinny opts for the Margin Scheme, GST @ 18% is levied on the positive margin.
  • If Spinny opts out of the Margin Scheme, GST @ 18% is charged on the sale price.

Further Sale by Cars24:

Under the Margin Scheme, Cars24 can both buy and sell used cars while paying GST only on the profit margin. However, if they claim input tax credit (ITC) on any used car purchase, they lose the eligibility to use the Margin Scheme for any subsequent sales.

GST Knowledge Check

Conclusion

The GST treatment of used car transactions depends on the registration status of the parties, the use of the Margin Scheme, and compliance with ITC rules. The Margin Scheme is beneficial for registered dealers to reduce tax liability by levying GST only on the positive margin, but it comes with the condition of not claiming ITC on purchases. Careful consideration of these provisions ensures compliance while optimizing the GST burden.
